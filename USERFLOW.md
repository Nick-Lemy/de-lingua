## DeLingua - User Flow

---

## Buyer Flow

### 1. Onboarding (One-Time Setup)

The buyer creates a smart profile by selecting structured preferences:

- Categories of interest (what they usually buy)
- Budget behavior (low-cost, quality-first, premium)
- Location radius (local, regional, nationwide)
- Value preferences (eco-friendly, local suppliers, fast delivery, etc.)

This information forms the foundation for future matches.

---

### 2. Home / Discovery Screen

The buyer lands on a dashboard designed for action, not conversation.  
They see:

- Suggested mission ideas
- Personalized discovery suggestions
- A clear **“Create Mission”** button

---

### 3. Create a Mission (Main Action)

The buyer defines a specific need through a guided form:

- Product or service needed
- Quantity
- Budget range
- Urgency
- Location constraint

Optional: an extra description field where AI helps structure the input in the background.

The buyer submits the mission to find matches.

---

### 4. Matching Results

The system presents ranked compatibility matches instead of search results.  
Each match includes:

- Match score or percentage
- Distance or location relevance
- Budget compatibility
- Current stock or production capacity
- Key attributes (e.g., eco-friendly, certified)

The system explains why each match fits the mission.

---

### 5. Seller Profile View

Before contacting the seller, the buyer can review:

- Product or service details
- Inventory levels
- Minimum order quantity
- Certifications
- Reviews or ratings
- Typical response time

---

### 6. Connect / Chat

Only after selecting a match does the buyer start a conversation to:

- Ask questions
- Negotiate
- Confirm order details

Chat is a support step, not the main experience.

---

## Seller Flow

### 1. Business Profile Setup

The seller defines structured business information:

- Business category
- Products or services
- Capabilities
- Minimum order quantities
- Location and service range
- Preferences and constraints

---

### 2. Inventory / Capacity Management

The seller maintains up-to-date operational data:

- Current stock levels
- Production or service capacity
- Lead times

This ensures buyers see real availability.

---

### 3. Passive Matching

The seller does not browse for buyers.  
They receive match notifications when a buyer mission fits their profile.

---

### 4. Connection

The seller reviews the buyer’s request and enters chat only after a match has been established.
